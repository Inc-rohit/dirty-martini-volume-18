Connected Retail Experience Study:
The New Omnichannel Reality

In partnership with Verizon and Cisco, we released our fifth annual study across grocery and specialty segments. AI is universally acknowledged as table stakes — 83% say it's necessary to compete — but only 6% consider their capabilities mature. The ambition-to-execution gap is real.

16%
satisfied with in-store personalization

The Personalization Paradox Is Getting Worse, Not Better

44% of retailers say in-store personalization is important, but only 16% are satisfied with what they're delivering. Drop into omnichannel and satisfaction falls to just 12%. Siloed data and disconnected systems continue to block progress.

What This Means for B2B Tech Marketing

If you sell anything that touches customer data unification, real-time decisioning, or cross-channel orchestration, this gap is your entire pitch. Lead with how you close the gap, not why it matters. Segment grocery vs. specialty — the pain is the same, the starting points are different.

2×
mobile tools for associates in two years

Retailers Are Betting on Associates, Not Just Algorithms

Mobile tools deployed for associates have doubled in the past two years. Push-to-talk, dedicated Wi-Fi, inventory apps, and video-based training are now table stakes. The focus has moved from hiring more people to making each associate more effective on the floor.

What This Means for B2B Tech Marketing

Associate enablement is the most active investment area in stores right now. If your solution touches workforce productivity, training, or real-time information access, this is your moment. The buyer is listening. Access to customer data on mobile is set to grow from 40% to 56% within two years.

68%
still exploring or planning AI strategy

AI Enthusiasm Is Universal. AI Execution Is Not.

The jump from planning to production stalls — complicated by poor or siloed data (55%), system integration challenges (48%), and shortage of specialized talent (44%). Grocers deploy AI against operations. Specialty retailers lean into customer-facing applications.

What This Means for B2B Tech Marketing

Stop leading with AI capabilities — start with data readiness. 56% of retailers rank foundational data infrastructure as their top AI investment priority. If you can help a retailer get their data house in order, you've earned the right to talk about what AI can do with it.

61%
report revenue impact from poor digital experiences

The Network Is the New Bottleneck

67% of network upgrades are now driven by AI and ML, yet only 39% of retailers are satisfied with their edge computing support, and 49% with managing peak traffic. Network performance is no longer an IT concern — it's a commercial one.

What This Means for B2B Tech Marketing

If you're in infrastructure, connectivity, or edge computing, this data reframes the conversation from cost-center to revenue-enabler. For non-infra vendors: your AI solution is only as good as the network it runs on. Partner messaging with connectivity providers isn't optional.

Dive Deeper

Dive deeper into our cross-industry research and content. This month, we released a new report — Unified Commerce in an AI World: What Breaks When Intelligence Outpaces Infrastructure.

Unified Commerce in an AI World

The AI experimentation phase is over. AI agents in 2026 don't just chat — they reason across product catalogs, negotiate fulfillment options, and execute purchases. MIT reports 95% of enterprise GenAI pilots fail to deliver ROI. Our research shows only 17% of retailers rate themselves as "Leading" in unified commerce maturity — and 84% are making decisions with incomplete information.

  • 1
    The AI MandateWhat separates leaders is whether AI can reason across unified, real-time data. When profiles, inventory, and fulfillment operate in silos, AI makes conservative choices and delivers limited impact.
  • 2
    AI Doesn't Fix Bad Data — It Industrializes ItLatency turns intelligence into risk. Most retail stacks weren't built for real-time unified data, forcing AI to act on partial truth. Neither guessing conservatively nor acting without full context is good.
  • 3
    The Unified Foundation IllusionIntegration moves data between systems but doesn't resolve conflicts. "Near real-time" hides reconciliation delays AI can't tolerate. Optimization happens locally rather than economically.
  • 4
    What AI-Ready Commerce Actually RequiresA single source of truth, real-time event streams (every click, sale, return processed as it happens), and zero-reconciliation integrity — eliminating the need for humans to determine which data is correct.
  • 5
    The Path ForwardFrom insight to prescriptive orchestration. The agentic shift: autonomous systems simulating thousands of scenarios in real time. The trust mandate: explainable AI with clear audit trails. Human roles evolve from executing decisions to governing models.

Straight from the Shaker

Why Partnerships Break — and How to Fix Them

This piece tackles the uncomfortable truth hiding inside most partner ecosystems: they look great in strategy decks and fall apart the moment a customer actually needs them to work together. KPMG reports 75% of executives view partner ecosystems as essential. Yet only 13% say their practices consistently deliver outcomes.

Problem 01

The Accountability Vacuum

68% of companies struggle with unclear role definition. If nobody owns the space between partners, nobody owns the customer outcome.

Problem 02

The Go-to-Market Gap

Sales teams rarely get the "better together" story. Budget falls into a gray area and the partnership quietly becomes a logo on a slide rather than a pipeline engine.

Problem 03

The Incentive Misalignment

71% cite aligning goals as the single biggest ecosystem challenge. Individual optimization creates fragmented outcomes for the buyer.

Solution 01

Invisible Infrastructure

Build shared workflows where teams operate as a single unit from the start. Customers shouldn't detect where one partner ends and another begins.

Solution 02

The Utility of Truth

Technical connectivity isn't operational integration. Customer intent, history, and context must travel seamlessly across partner boundaries.

Solution 03

Mutual Risk

Activity metrics don't drive execution. Set outcome-based measurement — milestone payouts tied to onboarding, joint bonuses triggered by measurable customer impact.

The bottom line: in a market where everyone has partnerships, competitive advantage belongs to those who make them actually work. The execution gap isn't inevitable. It's a design choice.
